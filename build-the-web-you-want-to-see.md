## Intro

We came a long way since Gutenberg invented mass media with the printing machine (1450). It's also been a while (1993) since Tim Berners-Lee published the first website introducing the world wide web. Where are we heading now?

Social media, no-code platforms and AI tools have made content creation easier ever before. Publishing your own website is a rather technical path. Is custom web development becoming a relic of the past?

## Walled gardens

> In our techno-feudalistic dystopia, they're the landlords. We're the serfs.

Attention economy: Social media silos and app stores have the eye balls. They also own the rules. When the algorithm sneezes, businesses catch a cold. Walled gardens are convenient as long as you comply.

Owning your content means hosting it on your own domain, controlling the URLs, and deciding how long it stays online.

## Quirky and naive

Looking at my filter bubble of web dev and web design, I see a renaissance of the naive web happening. Just like vinyl diggers, people are looking at ways to create expressive, naive and a quirky web, using open standards.

Handmade layouts, intentional asymmetry, stubborn color palettes, and copy that feels like it was written by a person. Keeping the web playful, opinionated, and weird in the best possible way.

## Open formats

Markdown is still a joy. RSS and WebSub still deliver posts to people who actually asked for them. Microformats (h-card, rel=\"me\", IndieAuth), Webmention, and ActivityPub glue personal sites together without yet another proprietary API. Backup your exports, publish in durable formats, and your words will outlive product sunsets.
